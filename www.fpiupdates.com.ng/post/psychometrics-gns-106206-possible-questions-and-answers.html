PSYCHOMETRICS (GNS 106/206) POSSIBLE QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS

This article is all about Psychometrics’ 20 Possible Questions and Answers for students that wants to prepare well and score good grades in their tests and exam. 

(1) The systematic use of psychological test is called…

  • Testing
  • Evaluation
  • Measurement
  • All of the above

 

(2) Is it possible that a norm established in Nigeria on male population can be different from female population?

  • Yes
  • No
  • I don’t know
  • It is not possible

 

(3) Enugu Somatization scale was developed by _______________

  • Ehigie
  • Ebigbo
  • Bakare
  • Awaritefe

 

(4) A standardization sample is a large representative sample of the type of persons for whom a test is designed, true or false?

  • True
  • False
  • Neither true not false
  • There is no standardization sample

 

(5) The main contribution of Sir Francis Galton in Psychometrics is that….

  • He established a Psychometrics lab
  • He invented a set of psychological measurements and form the prototypes for the standard equipment used in psychology laboratories today.
  • He introduced Psychometrics in higher institutions all over his country
  • None of the above

 

(6) What is the basic assumption of classical test theory?

  • True score does not exist therefore only error score are available
  • Observed score = true score plus some error
  • Error scores are more important
  • True scores do not exist

 

(7) Cognitive tests generally are designed to measure….

  • Mental abilities
  • Physical abilities
  • Emotional abilities
  • None of the above

 

(8) Objectivity, standardization and norm are all contained in…...

  • Test manual
  • Test developer
  • Test score
  • Test standardization

 

(9) Drug test is an example of the use of psychometric test in ….

  • Recreational centre
  • Rehabilitation centre
  • School setting
  • Tests cannot be used here

 

(10) Study Habit scale was developed by ______________

  • Ehigie
  • Ebigbo
  • Bakare
  • Awaritefe

 

(11) The degree to which a test results obtained by different scorers are the same, without influences of their biases or beliefs on scoring is called…

  • Projectivity
  • Objectivity
  • Ability test
  • None of the above

 

(12) One of the following statements contains all the 26 alphabets

  • The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog
  • The fast brown fox jumps over the lazy dog
  • The quick brown fox jumps on the lazy dog
  • None of the above

 

(13) The range of reliability if from …….to…….

  • .00 to 1.00
  • +1.00 to -1.00
  • .02 to .04
  • 0.3 – 0.7

 

(14) A good test reliability ranged from….to…. and above

  • .65 to .90
  • .50 to .65
  • .40 to .50
  • .30 to .40

 

(15) Vocational Interest Inventory (VII) was developed by….

  • Prof. B. O. Ehigie
  • Prof. Bakare
  • Prof. Osinowo
  • Dr. Adewumi

 

(16) A psychological test can best be used in clinical setting for

  • Diagnosis and referral
  • Knowing individual abilities
  • Selecting the best occupation for an individual
  • Attending to clients

 

(17) The uniformity of procedure in the administration and scoring of a test is called?

  • Objective tests
  • Standardization
  • Norm
  • Test re-test

 

(18) One of the following is not a concept in psychometrics

  • Validity
  • Testing
  • Standard Behaviour *
  • All of the above

 

Explanation: Basic Concepts in Psychometrics or Psychometrics properties are: Test, Testing, Measurement, Evaluation, Assessment, Validity, Reliability, Norm and Standardization.

 

(19) An instrument used in measuring a construct or sample of behaviour is called…

  • A test
  • An evaluator
  • A sample instrumental
  • None of the above

 

(20) Which of the following scoring requires respondents to select between a wide range of responses?

  • Continuum scoring
  • Dichotomous scoring
  • Scaling scoring *
  • All of the above
